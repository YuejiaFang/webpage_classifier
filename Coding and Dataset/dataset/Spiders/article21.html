5 of the world’s most amazing spiders

 

A female green lynx spider. (Bert Cash/Flickr/CC BY)

You either like them or you hate them. Spiders are some of the most unique and fascinating creatures on the planet.

Other than the oceans, you can find them almost anywhere on the planet. Some of them have developed the most amazing ways to hunt, mate and hide from predators.

Let’s take a look at just five of these amazing — and yes, creepy — creatures.

Peacock spider

A peacock spider showing his colours. (Photo by Jurgen Otto licensed CC BY-SA 2.0)

These amazing looking spiders are found in Australia. Their little dance might look like they’re getting ready to attack an enemy. They actually raise their colourful abdomens and boogie down in order to attract a mate.

These tiny little spiders use their colourful bodies to impress female spiders. You’ll have a hard time seeing them. They’re only five millimetres long. That's so small they could fit on your thumbnail!

Spiny Orb Weaver

Photo by giovzaid85 licensed CC BY 2.0

You can find these horned spiders all over the world. Even though they look pretty mean, they’re actually really harmless.

The crab-like horns and spines on their backs are meant to deter predators. The spiny orb weaver is only about five to nine millimetres long. They’re probably quite hard to see. If you look really hard, you might find one in your garden.

Jumping spider

A jumping spider on a leaf. (Wikimedia/Nick Hobgood/CC BY-SA)

The jumping spider is the easiest to identify because of its unique square-shaped flat face. They have 4 sets of eyes. The two in the front are much larger than all the others.

Most of these spiders can jump several times the length of their bodies. Before they jump, they attach a strand of silk to whatever they're standing on. That way if the jump doesn't work out, they can climb back up to where they started.

Jumping spiders feed on insects. But there is one species that is vegetarian and feeds on plants.

Bird dung crab spider

That white blob that looks like bird poop is actually a little spider. (Jean and Fred/Flickr/CC BY)

You’re probably thinking that the bird dung crab spider must really stink. You’re right. But think of how smart this spider is.

Its body is covered in warts and blobs that look like bird dung and it smells like poop. With this yucky camouflage predators aren’t interested in eating it.

And as a bonus, its camouflage attract the moths that are its favourite food. You can find these spiders in Asia.

Green lynx spider

A female green lynx spider protecting her eggsac. (Marshal Hedin/Flickr/CC BY-SA)

The green lynx spider gets its name from its bright green colour. They also move fast, jumping from place to place like a lynx. Their legs are covered with bristle-like hairs called setae making them look spiny.

They don’t spin a web, instead they jump into the air to catch insects in flight. The female spider is bigger than the male at about 16 millimetres in length. That's about the size of a stack of 11 pennies.

Just watch out. These spiders aren’t poisonous but they do bite! You can find these spiders in the southeastern United States and Mexico.
